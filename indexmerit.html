MERIT BASED SCHOLARSHIPS

Australia Awards Scholarships

Merit-based, fully funded scholarships for students from developing nations.

Overview: The Australia Awards Scholarships offer opportunities for individuals from developing countries to undertake full-time undergraduate or postgraduate study at participating Australian universities and Technical and Further Education (TAFE) institutions.

Eligibility: Must be a citizen of a participating country, demonstrate academic potential, and meet specific eligibility criteria for their country and chosen field of study.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, return air travel, establishment allowance, contribution to living expenses, Overseas Student Health Cover, and supplementary academic support.

Application: Requires an online application, supporting documents, and may include interviews. Deadlines vary by country (usually between January and April).

ETH Zurich Excellence Scholarship

Merit-based financial aid for top master's students at ETH Zurich.

Overview: The ETH Zurich Excellence Scholarship & Opportunity Programme (ESOP) supports excellent master's students with a scholarship, mentorship, and networking opportunities.

Eligibility: Requires excellent performance in bachelor's studies, admission to a master's program at ETH Zurich, and demonstrated motivation.

Benefits: Full or partial coverage of living and study expenses, tuition fee waiver, and mentorship.

Application: Involves applying for the master's program and a separate scholarship application. Deadlines vary by program (usually November-December).

Visit ETH Zurich Excellence Scholarship Website

Schwarzman Scholars (China)

Fully funds a master's degree at Tsinghua University in China.

Overview: The Schwarzman Scholars program is a fully funded master's program at Tsinghua University in Beijing, designed to build a global network of future leaders who can deepen understanding between China and the rest of the world.

Eligibility: Requires a bachelor's degree, English proficiency, and demonstrated leadership potential. Open to students from all countries.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, room and board, travel to and from Beijing, an in-country study tour, required course materials, health insurance, and a stipend for personal expenses.

Application: Involves an online application, essays, recommendations, and interviews. Deadlines vary by region (usually in September).

Visit Schwarzman Scholars Website

Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships

Provides funding to top doctoral students with leadership potential.

Overview: The Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships (Vanier CGS) program aims to attract and retain world-class doctoral students by offering support to those who demonstrate both academic excellence and leadership potential.

Eligibility: Must be nominated by a Canadian university, be pursuing a doctoral degree in a health research, natural sciences and/or engineering, or social sciences and humanities field, and have a strong academic record.

Benefits: $50,000 per year for up to three years.

Application: Requires nomination by a Canadian university. Deadlines vary by faculty and university (usually between September and November).

Visit Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships Website

AAUW International Fellowship (USA)

Supports women pursuing graduate or postgraduate study in the U.S.

Overview: The AAUW (American Association of University Women) International Fellowships are awarded for full-time study or research in the United States to women who are not U.S. citizens or permanent residents.

Eligibility: Must have a bachelor's degree, be a woman with citizenship in a country other than the U.S., and intend to return to their home country to pursue a professional career.

Benefits: Varies depending on the level of study ($20,000 for Master's, $25,000 for PhD, and $50,000 for postdoctoral).

Application: Requires an online application, essays, and recommendations. Deadline is usually in November.

Visit AAUW International Fellowship Website

Gates Cambridge Scholarship

Covers tuition and living expenses for exceptional students at Cambridge.

Overview: The Gates Cambridge Scholarships are awarded to outstanding applicants from countries outside the UK to pursue a full-time postgraduate degree in any subject available at the University of Cambridge.

Eligibility: Must be applying to pursue a postgraduate degree at the University of Cambridge, have outstanding intellectual ability, leadership potential, and a commitment to improving the lives of others.

Benefits: Full cost of studying at Cambridge, including tuition fees, maintenance allowance, airfare, and discretionary funding.

Application: Involves applying for admission to the University of Cambridge and a separate Gates Cambridge application. Deadlines vary by department (usually in December or January).

Visit Gates Cambridge Scholarship Website

TaiwanICDF Scholarship

Fully funds international students for undergraduate and graduate programs.

Overview: The TaiwanICDF (International Cooperation and Development Fund) Scholarship Program provides full scholarships for selected international students to pursue undergraduate or graduate studies in Taiwan.

Eligibility: Must be a citizen of a partner country, meet specific academic requirements, and be proficient in English or Chinese (depending on the program).

Benefits: Full tuition fees, accommodation, living allowance, round-trip airfare, and insurance.

Application: Requires an online application and submission of documents through the Taiwan embassy or representative office. Deadlines vary by country (usually between January and March).

Visit TaiwanICDF Scholarship Website

Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's Scholarship

Funds talented students for master's programs at multiple European universities.

Overview: The Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's Degrees (EMJMD) are high-level integrated international study programs delivered by an international consortium of higher education institutions from different countries worldwide. The EU funds these programs to support talented students.

Eligibility: Requires a bachelor's degree or equivalent, and must meet the specific admission requirements of the chosen EMJMD program. Open to students from all countries.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, monthly allowance for living costs, travel and installation costs, and insurance coverage.

Application: Requires applying directly to the chosen Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's Degree program. Deadlines vary by program (usually between January and March).
