MINORITY BASED SCHOLARSHIPS

Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program

Fully funds African students from disadvantaged backgrounds for undergraduate and graduate study.

Overview: The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program provides access to quality education for young people from economically disadvantaged communities in Africa, enabling them to become leaders and drive change in their communities.

Eligibility: Must be a citizen of an African country, demonstrate academic talent, leadership potential, and a commitment to giving back to their communities. Financial need is also a key criterion.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, accommodation, living expenses, travel, and comprehensive support services.

Application: Requires application through partner universities, with specific requirements and deadlines varying by institution.

Visit Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program Website

Aga Khan Foundation International Scholarship

Provides need-based scholarships to students from select developing countries.

Overview: The Aga Khan Foundation International Scholarship Programme provides a limited number of need-based scholarships each year for postgraduate studies to outstanding students from select developing countries.

Eligibility: Must be a national of a developing country where AKF has programmes, have a bachelor’s degree, and demonstrate financial need. Preference is given to students who have obtained admission to reputable universities.

Benefits: Tuition fees and living expenses, provided as a 50% grant and 50% loan.

Application: Requires application through AKF offices in their country of residence. Deadlines vary by country.

Visit Aga Khan Foundation International Scholarship Website

Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship

Supports students from developing countries pursuing graduate studies in development-related fields.

Overview: The Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program (JJ/WBGSP) supports students from developing countries to pursue graduate studies in development-related fields at participating universities worldwide.

Eligibility: Must be a citizen of a developing World Bank member country, have a bachelor’s degree, and have at least three years of development-related work experience. Must be admitted to a participating master's program.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, living allowance, round-trip air travel, and health insurance.

Application: Requires application through participating universities, with specific requirements and deadlines varying by institution.

Visit Joint Japan/World Bank Graduate Scholarship Website

American Association of University Women

Provides financial support to women pursuing graduate studies in the U.S.

Overview: The American Association of University Women (AAUW) offers fellowships and grants to women pursuing graduate and postgraduate studies in the United States.

Eligibility: Must be a woman with a bachelor’s degree, pursuing a graduate degree in the U.S. Specific requirements vary by fellowship.

Benefits: Varies depending on the fellowship, ranging from $20,000 to $50,000.

Application: Requires an online application with essays and recommendations. Deadlines vary by fellowship (usually in November).

Visit AAUW Fellowships and Grants Website

AAUW International Fellowship (USA)

Supports women pursuing graduate or postgraduate study in the U.S.

Overview: The AAUW International Fellowships are awarded for full-time study or research in the United States to women who are not U.S. citizens or permanent residents.

Eligibility: Must have a bachelor's degree, be a woman with citizenship in a country other than the U.S., and intend to return to their home country to pursue a professional career.

Benefits: Varies depending on the level of study ($20,000 for Master's, $25,000 for PhD, and $50,000 for postdoctoral).

Application: Requires an online application, essays, and recommendations. Deadline is usually in November.

Visit AAUW International Fellowship Website

PEO International Peace Scholarship

Supports international women students for graduate studies in the U.S. and Canada.

Overview: The P.E.O. International Peace Scholarship Fund (IPS) is a program that provides scholarships for international women students to pursue graduate studies in the United States and Canada.

Eligibility: Must be a woman from a country other than the U.S. or Canada, have a bachelor’s degree, and be accepted into a graduate program in the U.S. or Canada.

Benefits: Up to $12,500.

Application: Requires application through a P.E.O. chapter. Deadlines vary.

Visit PEO International Peace Scholarship Website

Turkiye Burslari Scholarship

Prioritizes students from underrepresented regions and developing countries.

Overview: The Türkiye Bursları Scholarship is a government-funded scholarship program that provides opportunities for international students to study in Turkey at undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral levels.

Eligibility: Must be a citizen of a country other than Turkey, meet age restrictions, and fulfill academic requirements. Priority is given to students from underrepresented regions and developing countries.

Benefits: Full tuition fees, accommodation, monthly stipend, travel allowance, and health insurance.

Application: Requires an online application. Deadline is usually in February.

Visit Turkiye Burslari Scholarship Website

Offers funding to outstanding minority students in the U.S. pursuing undergraduate and graduate studies.

Overview: The Gates Millennium Scholars (GMS) Program was a scholarship program funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation that provided scholarships to outstanding minority students with significant financial need to attend any college or university of their choice.
